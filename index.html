Log Analysis

What do you look for when you're looking at logs?

I look for outliers. I want filter out the log entries that are noisy, and look for the unusual ones.

With Unix, first and foremost, I filter out the log entries that I'm not intested in. cat log | grep -v 'boring'. When that's not enough, I cluster the log entries by removing their uniqueness. Something like: cat log | tr '[0-9]' N | sort | uniq -c | sort -n That produces a histogram of log clusters.

The next step is to visualize those clusters over time, and that's what this page does. It clusters log entries by text (using the simple mechanism of removing numbers) and then by time (building a histogram).
